The honest read

Best path right now

The VR catalog is evergreen locked and unforgiving to new paid games. Across all of 2025, only about four VR only paid titles passed 100,000 copies on Steam, and not one 2026 release has cracked the Quest top 50. Two realistic paths remain for a newcomer:

  1. A cheap, VR native novelty. A sharp quirky sim at the $10 to $20 price point, or a co-op horror title with a distinct hook. Low cost, fast, concept driven. The best risk adjusted bet.
  2. A social or physics F2P hook. Aimed at the teen social audience. The highest ceiling in VR, but it is a live service company and a virality lottery, not a project.

Everything else (AAA narrative, competitive shooters, fitness subscriptions) is capital intensive, exposed to platform retreat, or both. Whichever genre you pick, pair it with a cross platform plan given how small each base is.

Pinpointed path: build for Quest 3 and 3S plus SteamVR for reach, and make a quirky VR native sim or a co-op horror title with one strong original hook.

Built by a developer, for developers

Why this exists

I have spent more than 25 years building and releasing games and simulations across consoles, PC, VR, XR, and proprietary hardware. I have shipped award winning titles with teams that ranged from a handful of people to several hundred, and I have founded and run studios along the way.

In 2024 I released Nope Challenge, a VR passion project. I was not chasing a blockbuster. I was chasing something I genuinely wanted to make, and it taught me more as a developer, designer, manager, and creative lead than almost any project before it. It reviewed well. But reviews do not make payroll, and even a passion project needs enough sales to keep a team fed and working.

That is the real problem this tool helps with. It is not here to make the decision for you. It is here to augment it, to give a developer firmer footing for a call only they can make. VR is a wild, narrow market kept alive by a small group of genuinely great people. I have a long list of ideas I believe would be a joy to build and could reach a far wider audience than most VR titles ever do. The hard part is not the idea. It is the path. Which platform, which genre, which bet actually brings in enough to support a healthy small team without risking the studio.

So I built a compass. Something that cuts through the noise and points at the next sound move. If it helps me choose well, it should help you choose well too. That is the whole point.
