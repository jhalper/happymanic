Friendslop Idea Page

Friendslop: cheap, goofy co-op games built around playing with friends — Lethal Company, PEAK, R.E.P.O. The genre's rule is thin systems, tuned precisely, so the players generate the content. Four original concepts below, graded against the must-haves. Every grade is my own pre-prototype call — tell me where I'm wrong.

How to comment: each concept has a code — PP Porch Pirates, SH Shear, BO Bounce, AD After Dark — and criteria are numbered C1–C8, so you can just say AD-C5 feels generous. Tap any concept card for the full pitch.

The Concepts

Porch Pirates PP "Winning exposes you." ▸ Read the pitch▾ Close

A sunny sitcom suburb on delivery day. Most players are homeowners guarding packages; a hidden few are pirates. Stealing a package instantly transforms you — a sea shanty plays from your body, spatialized so anyone nearby can hunt you by sound, and a slapstick chase to a flying pirate ship begins. Drop the loot to revert. Anyone can call an HOA meeting to vote a suspect off the street.

Why it works: the win action and the getting-caught action are the same act. Greed literally makes noise, so the tension generates itself.

Questions for you
  1. Which sounds more fun: pirate or homeowner? If one side is boring, the game is broken.
  2. Does the HOA vote add drama, or slow down a chase game?
Shear SH "The product fights back." ▸ Read the pitch▾ Close

You and your friends run a wool farm. The livestock have not agreed to the business plan. Market days run 10–15 minutes: corral physics-driven sheep, alpacas and llamas that resist, flee and spit; shear them (the fluffy-to-naked reveal is the signature gag); the machine and shop run themselves. Demand rises, unsheared animals grow into rolling wool boulders, and the roster gets weirder every round.

Why it works: the goal has opinions. Every dollar earned is a physical argument with an animal — nobody scripts the llama.

Questions for you
  1. Can uncooperative animals alone — no monsters — hold your group for 30 minutes?
  2. Funniest pressure engine: wool overgrowth, demand spikes, or breeding runaway?
Bounce BO "You don't jump — you get jumped." ▸ Read the pitch▾ Close

The camp blob. One player lies on the end of the giant lake inflatable; a friend jumps from the tower onto the other end and launches them — at targets, at hanging grabs, over the croc line, onto the next platform. Mid-air you're a steerable ragdoll. Chain blobs and the group leapfrogs the whole lake, where one flop strands everyone. Solo, the mechanic flips Angry Birds-style: you're the jumper, launching dummies and an inflatable flamingo at targets.

Why it works: your friend's timing decides your flight. Every launch is a trust fall at 40 feet — and deliberately bad jumps are perfectly legal.

Questions for you
  1. Honestly — would you play this solo?
  2. Is legal sabotage funny in your group, or does it start fights?
Bounce: After Dark AD "Every escape needs a volunteer." ▸ Read the pitch▾ Close

A summer-camp slasher movie where the blob is your only way out. Days are the hangout lake above — goofing off, targets, solo slingshot play. Nights, something hunts the camp: a lethal "NO RUNNING" lifeguard, a mascot with something wrong inside. The lake is off-limits (something lives in it), so you can't swim — only fly. Launches are loud, and every splash announces your position. The launcher can't launch themselves, so every escape needs a volunteer to stay behind. Someone is always last.

Why it works: the fun thing is the dangerous thing — the mechanic you want to use is the dinner bell. And who stays behind is a social negotiation no designer has to write.

Questions for you
  1. The volunteer mechanic: fun tension, or just stressful?
  2. Does the wholesome-skin killer land as funny-scary, or defang the horror?

Time To Prototype — the one question each build must answer

Bounce
1 week
Does the launch feel good? Two ragdolls, one blob, water. If testers laugh with zero content, greenlight. Gates both Bounce renditions.
Shear
2 weeks
Are the sheep funny? One field, three animals with resist behaviors, one shear tool.
After Dark
3 weeks
Does a launch under pursuit create panic-then-laughter? Blob test plus one dumb chaser and a night state.
Porch Pirates
4–6 wks
Does the reveal-and-chase create real panic? Roles, transformation and the shanty must exist before the fun is testable.

Enhancement Paths

Bounce

  • Leapfrog traversal — chain blobs across the lake; one flop strands the team
  • Mid-air ragdoll steering — flail, reach, grab; no QTE prompts
  • Solo slingshot flip — launch dummies and the flamingo at targets
  • Hazards as geography — croc line, piranha shallows; arc over them, not events
  • HORSE mode — call your shot; loser wears the towel cape

After Dark

  • Day/night structure — days are the sandbox and tutorial; nights are survival
  • Projectile roster — heavy camper cannonball, skinny-kid glider, floatie-kid water-skipper
  • Camp dog launcher — solo nights: whistle and the dog jumps… unless a duck
  • The volunteer problem — the launcher stays behind; the group decides who
  • Guard rail — day is Angry Birds, night is Lethal Company; keep night thin

Shear

  • Collapse the chain — automate machine and shelves; keep friction in the animals
  • Escalating weirdness — angrier rams, a llama with a grudge, an emu incursion
  • Wool overgrowth — unsheared animals become rolling boulders
  • Customers in the sandbox — spooked, spat-on NPCs pay differently
  • Wear what you make — the shop is the wardrobe

Porch Pirates

  • Cosmetics on the pirate form — spend where the camera lives: the chase
  • Daily map rotation — the cheap replay trick PEAK proved
  • Built-in clip capture — auto-record the reveal; the clip is the marketing
  • Deterrent sandbox — glitter bombs, sprinklers, decoys
  • HOA drama tools — accusation emotes, petty fines, a gavel
The read: Bounce and After Dark are the same bet at two scopes — the week-one blob test gates both. After Dark trades scope for an Extreme pressure curve, solo viability, and the strongest hook of the four. Shear has the best raw comedy and the weakest structure; Porch Pirates has the deepest hooks at the highest cost. Rational order: blob test → day-only vs day/night → Shear → Porch Pirates. What helps most: which of these you'd actually boot with your group on a Friday night — and which you'd skip, and why.
